The "Modular Analyst" Workflow
You can master the functions of Security Onion without the weight of Security Onion. Here is how you split the functions:
1. Focus: Network Attacks (The "Suricata" Phase)
The Goal: Detect Nmap scans or malicious traffic.
The "Lite" Method: Run your Kali/Parrot VM and your Host. Use Wireshark on your host to capture the "attack" traffic.
What you skip: Data integrity. You are focusing purely on the "Wire."
2. Focus: Data Integrity (The "Wazuh" Phase)
The Goal: Detect if a file was changed (FIM).
The "Lite" Method: Run your Windows 10 VM and your Splunk Host. Run your PowerShell hashing script or Sysmon.
What you skip: Network scanning. You are focusing purely on the "Disk."
3. Focus: Log Analysis (The "Elastic/Splunk" Phase)
The Goal: Searching and correlating.
The "Lite" Method: Turn off all VMs. Just run Splunk on your host and analyze the data you collected in the previous steps.
What you skip: Real-time generation. You are focusing purely on the "Intelligence."
